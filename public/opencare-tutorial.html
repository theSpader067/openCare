Creating a New Patient

Lab Tests & Analyses

Manage laboratory tests, imaging requests, and pathology results. Order tests, track results, and use our built-in OCR technology to extract data from physical reports. Everything stays organized by patient and chronological order.

Analyses List & OCR Scanner
[Insert analyses/lab management screenshot here]

Analysis Categories

Bilan (Lab Tests)
Blood work, urinalysis, and other laboratory tests. Track results, compare trends, and add clinical notes.
Imagerie (Imaging)
X-rays, CT scans, MRI, ultrasound, and other imaging studies. Store reports and images with interpretations.
Anapath (Pathology)
Tissue samples, biopsies, and pathological analyses. Keep detailed pathology reports linked to patients.
Autres (Other)
Any other specialized tests or analyses that don't fit the above categories.

Using the OCR Scanner

Automatically extract text and data from physical medical reports using our intelligent OCR technology:

1
Open Scanner
Click the camera/OCR icon in the Analyses section to open the scanner modal.
2
Upload Medical Document
Take a photo or upload an image of the medical report (supports JPG, PNG, PDF).
3
Automatic Processing
OpenCare automatically compresses the image (80%+ reduction) and extracts all text using OCR.
4
Review & Save
Review the extracted text, make corrections if needed, add clinical notes, then save to patient record.
OCR Advantages
  • Saves time manually typing reports
  • Reduces data entry errors
  • Automatic image compression
  • Searchable text in patient records
  • Organized chronologically
  • Easy to share with team
‚ö° Time-Saver: The OCR feature can save you 5-10 minutes per report by automatically extracting and organizing lab values and imaging findings.

Tips & Best Practices

Get the most out of OpenCare with these practical tips and workflow optimization strategies. These best practices will help you work more efficiently and provide better patient care.

Daily Workflow Tips

1
Start with Dashboard
Review your dashboard each morning to see today's tasks, upcoming activities, and pending work items. Prioritize high-priority tasks first.
2
Check Patient Timeline Before Visits
Before seeing a patient, review their timeline to stay informed about their recent medical history and current status.
3
Document Immediately
Add observations and notes during or immediately after patient visits while information is fresh.
4
Use Prescription Templates
Create templates for your most common prescriptions. This saves time and reduces errors.
5
Assign Tasks for Follow-ups
When a patient needs follow-up care, create a task immediately and assign it to ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

Efficiency Boosters

Quick Wins for Productivity
  • Use OCR scanner to digitize medical reports quickly
  • Create prescription templates for common medications
  • Set up favorite filters for quick searches
  • Use keyboard shortcuts for navigation
  • Batch similar tasks together (e.g., all prescriptions, all consultations)
  • Delegate tasks to team members appropriately
  • Review analytics monthly to track your productivity
  • Archive or delete completed tasks regularly

Data Entry Best Practices

Completeness
Fill in all relevant fields when creating patient records and documents. Complete records are more useful for continuity of care.
Accuracy
Double-check dosages, dates, and patient identifiers. Errors in clinical data can have serious consequences.
Consistency
Use consistent terminology and abbreviations. This makes records easier for you and your team to understand.
Timeliness
Enter data promptly while details are fresh. Delayed documentation can lead to errors and forgotten information.

Team Collaboration Best Practices

1
Clear Task Descriptions
When assigning tasks, provide clear descriptions and context so team members understand what needs to be done.
2
Regular Check-ins
Review team tasks and progress regularly. Use OpenCare's notifications to stay informed about team activity.
3
Shared Templates
Create shared prescription templates and observation templates that your whole team can use for consistency.
4
Patient Access
Grant appropriate team members access to shared patients. More eyes can catch important details.
üéØ Success Metric: If your team is using OpenCare effectively, you should see reduced time spent on documentation, fewer follow-up delays, and improved communication about patient care.
